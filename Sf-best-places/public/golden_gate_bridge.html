GOLDEN GATE BRIDGE

History

Welcome to the Golden Gate Bridge, the pinnacle of architectural beauty and engineering feat. This famous suspension bridge, which stands tall and majestic against the backdrop of San Francisco Bay, represents human ingenuity and inventiveness more than just connecting the city and its surrounding counties. The bridge, which spans 1.7 miles and spans the Golden Gate Strait, draws attention from onlookers with its imposing orange towers and arching cables. The California sun's warm rays or the city's renowned fog never cease to enthrall viewers with the Golden Gate Bridge's eternal charm.

You will be in complete amazement at the size of this architectural wonder when you cross its famous span. Every moment spent on the Golden Gate Bridge is an incredible experience that will always remain etched in your heart and spirit, from the exhilarating stroll across its pedestrian pathways to the magnificent vistas of the Pacific Ocean and San Francisco skyline.

Getting there

  • By Car: Accessible via US Highway 101 and State Route 1. Parking is available at both the north and south ends of the bridge.
  • Public Transportation: Buses from San Francisco and Marin County offer routes directly to the bridge.

Visitor Facilities

  • Battery Spencer: A historic military fort offering panoramic views of the bridge.
  • Fort Point: Located under the southern end of the bridge, offering unique views.

Visitor Information

  • Hours: Open 24/7, 365 days a year.
  • Admission: Free to walk or bike across. Vehicle tolls apply.
  • Facilities: Visitor center, gift shop, restrooms available.
